# Variant Prompts — Creative Concepts for Haggle

> 5 unconventional website concepts. Each one IS the product experience, not a description of it.

---

## CONCEPT 1 — "The Negotiation Game"

> The visitor IS the buyer. They negotiate against Haggle's AI live on the website. The landing page is the product demo.

### Prompt 1-1: The Opening Screen

```
A full-screen dark landing page that looks like a game start screen.

Center of screen: a product card showing an iPhone 15 Pro with a price tag "$950".
Below it: a large glowing text input field labeled "What would you pay?" with a blinking cursor.

Above the product: "ROUND 1" in small uppercase monospace text.
Subtle particle effects in the background — like embers floating.

No navigation, no header. Just the product, the question, and a dark void.
At the very bottom, tiny text: "Type a number. Let's see if you can beat our AI."

Vibe: like the opening screen of a text adventure game. Mysterious, inviting, slightly challenging. The user doesn't know what happens next — they just have to type.
```

### Prompt 1-2: The Negotiation Round

```
After the user types a price, the screen splits into a negotiation interface.

Left side: User's offer in a blue card — "$720"
Right side: AI's counter in an amber card — "$880"
Center: a vertical line with a pulsing dot that moves between the two prices.

Below: a new input field — "Your counter?"
Round indicator: "ROUND 2 of ?"

The background subtly shifts color as rounds progress — getting warmer (more tense) as you get closer to a deal, or cooler if you're far apart.

Small annotations appear like game tips:
"TIP: The AI conceded 7.4%. You're getting closer."
"The seller's floor is somewhere below $850..."

No other UI elements. Just the negotiation. Immersive.
```

### Prompt 1-3: The Deal Screen

```
The moment a deal is reached — a celebration screen.

The two price cards fly toward each other and merge into one green card showing the deal price: "$810"

Confetti or particle burst animation. Screen flashes briefly.

Stats appear one by one with count-up animation:
"You saved: $140 (14.7%)"
"Rounds: 4"
"Time: 38 seconds"
"Difficulty: Medium"

Below: "On eBay, you would have paid $950 and the seller would get $827 after fees."
Then: "On Haggle, you paid $810 and the seller gets $798. Everyone wins."

CTA: "That was a demo. Ready for real deals? → Join Haggle"

Secondary link: "Try again with a different product →"
```

## CONCEPT 2 — "The Money Museum"

> Each "room" in the museum shows a different way traditional marketplaces take your money. You walk through doors to explore. The final room is Haggle.

### Prompt 2-1: The Entrance Hall

```
A museum-style landing page. Dark elegant background — think art gallery at night.

Center: A grand entrance with the text "THE MUSEUM OF LOST MARGINS" in gold serif font.

Below: "A guided tour of where your money goes when you sell online."

Two ornate doors:
- Left door: "The eBay Room" — a warm red glow leaks from under the door
- Right door: "The Poshmark Room" — a purple glow

Or a single door: "Enter the Museum →"

Vibe: dramatic, like entering an escape room or an art installation. Not a website — an experience.
At the bottom, tiny: "Curated by Haggle. Takes 2 minutes."
```

### Prompt 2-2: The eBay Room

```
A museum room with three framed "artworks" on the wall. Each frame shows a fee being taken.

Frame 1 (large, center): "The Commission" — an animated visualization of $450 being reduced. The number $59.63 peels off and floats into an eBay logo. Title plaque below: "13.25% Final Value Fee — eBay, est. 1995"

Frame 2 (smaller, left): "The Processing" — $13.35 dissolving into a PayPal logo. Plaque: "2.9% + $0.30 — The invisible tax"

Frame 3 (smaller, right): "The Shipping" — a shipping label being stamped with $8.00. Plaque: "Seller-subsidized shipping — The expectation trap"

Floor plaque: "Total extracted from a $450 sale: $80.98. Artist received: $369.02."

A door on the right wall: "Next Room →" with a green glow (leading to Haggle room).
A door on the left: "← The Poshmark Room" with purple glow.

Style: dark walls, spotlight lighting on each frame, museum-quality typography. The frames should look like real artworks — gilded edges, subtle shadows.
```

### Prompt 2-3: The Haggle Room

```
The final museum room. Unlike the others, this room is bright — white/green light.

One single frame on the wall, but it's a mirror. The reflection shows YOUR money coming back.

In the mirror/frame: "$450 sale → $443.25 received"
Plaque: "The Haggle Protocol — 1.5% total fee. Non-custodial. You keep 98.5%."

Beside the mirror, a comparison display:
"You just walked through $80.98 in fees (eBay Room) and $90.00 in fees (Poshmark Room). Here, it's $6.75."

The room has no other doors — just a large CTA in the center:
"Exit the Museum. Enter the Future. → Start Selling on Haggle"

Style: the contrast should be striking. Dark, heavy museum rooms → this bright, minimal room. The message: Haggle is the escape from the old world.
```

## CONCEPT 3 — "The Price Tug-of-War"

> A single interactive visualization. Two sides pulling a rope. The user controls one side with their mouse/scroll.

### Prompt 3-1: The Tug of War

```
A full-screen interactive landing page. No traditional sections.

Center: a horizontal rope stretched across the screen.
Left side: "BUYER" in blue — a figure or icon pulling left.
Right side: "SELLER" in amber — a figure or icon pulling right.
Center of rope: a flag with a price on it — "$850"

The user scrolls or drags to pull the price in either direction:
- Scroll down / drag left → price drops toward buyer's ideal
- Scroll up / drag right → price rises toward seller's ideal

As you pull:
- The background color shifts (blue tint ← → amber tint)
- Fee breakdowns appear on each side:
  Left: "Buyer pays: $812 (including 0.75% Haggle fee)"
  Right: "Seller receives: $806 (after 0.75% Haggle fee)"
- A comparison ghost text: "On eBay, seller would get $741 at this price"

When you find equilibrium (center zone), the rope goes slack and text appears:
"This is what fair looks like. Both sides chose this price."

CTA appears: "Let your AI find this point automatically → Try Haggle"

Vibe: physical, tactile. Like a playground game but with real financial stakes. Simple enough that anyone understands immediately.
```

## CONCEPT 4 — "The Receipt Shredder"

> You upload (or we show) a fake eBay/Mercari receipt. The page visually shreds the fees away, revealing what you SHOULD have kept.

### Prompt 4-1: The Receipt

```
Landing page that looks like a crumpled receipt on a dark surface.

The receipt shows a typical eBay sale:
  Item: iPhone 15 Pro
  Sale Price: $450.00
  ─────────────────
  eBay Fee:        -$59.63
  Payment Process: -$13.35
  Promoted Listing: -$9.00
  Shipping Label:  -$8.50
  ─────────────────
  YOU RECEIVED:    $359.52

The fees are highlighted in red, pulsing slightly.

Headline above: "Does this look familiar?"

Below the receipt: a button that says "Shred the Fees →"

When clicked (or on scroll): the receipt goes through an animated paper shredder.
The red fee lines get shredded and disappear.
A new clean receipt prints out below:

  Item: iPhone 15 Pro
  Sale Price: $450.00
  ─────────────────
  Haggle Fee:      -$6.75
  Shipping:        -$4.99
  ─────────────────
  YOU RECEIVED:    $438.26

The new receipt is clean, green-tinted, and satisfying.

Headline: "Same item. Same buyer. $78.74 more in your pocket."
CTA: "Stop getting shredded. → Start Selling on Haggle"
```

## CONCEPT 5 — "The Time Machine"

> A timeline that shows the history of selling fees — from garage sales (0%) to eBay (13%) to Haggle (1.5%). The user scrolls through time.

### Prompt 5-1: The Timeline

```
A horizontal scrolling timeline landing page. Dark background, timeline running left to right.

Each era is a "scene" you scroll through:

Scene 1 — 1990s: "The Garage Sale"
- Illustration: a hand-drawn yard sale sign
- Fee: 0%
- Text: "You set the price. Buyer haggles face to face. Fee: your Saturday morning."

Scene 2 — 1995: "eBay Is Born"
- Illustration: the old eBay logo, a browser window
- Fee: 5% → grew to 13%
- Text: "Revolutionary at first. Then the fees crept up. And up. And up."

Scene 3 — 2011: "The App Marketplaces"
- Illustration: phone screens showing Poshmark, Mercari, Depop logos
- Fee: 10-20%
- Text: "Pretty apps. Ugly fees. They said 'we connect buyers and sellers.' They meant 'we take a fifth of everything.'"

Scene 4 — 2023: "The AI Moment"
- Illustration: a chat bubble with an AI icon
- Fee: still 10-20%
- Text: "AI got smart. Marketplaces didn't get cheaper. The technology changed. The business model didn't."

Scene 5 — 2026: "Haggle"
- Illustration: the Haggle interface, glowing green
- Fee: 1.5%
- Text: "AI negotiation. On-chain settlement. 1.5% total. The fee that actually makes sense."

The visual style shifts as you scroll: hand-drawn sketchy for 90s → pixel art for early 2000s → flat design for 2010s → glassmorphism for 2020s → clean dark premium for Haggle.

Final frame: full-screen CTA
"The future of selling is here. And it costs 1.5%."
"→ Start Selling on Haggle"
```

## RECOMMENDATIONS

| Concept | Best For | Complexity | Impact |
|---------|----------|------------|--------|
| 1. Negotiation Game | Product demo, viral | High | 🔥🔥🔥 |
| 2. Money Museum | Storytelling, emotional | Medium | 🔥🔥 |
| 3. Tug-of-War | Simplicity, one idea | Low | 🔥🔥 |
| 4. Receipt Shredder | Pain point, instant clarity | Medium | 🔥🔥🔥 |
| 5. Time Machine | Context, history buffs | Medium | 🔥 |

**My recommendation**: Start with **Concept 1 (Negotiation Game)** — it IS the product. Visitors experience Haggle before they even sign up. Then use **Concept 4 (Receipt Shredder)** as a secondary page or section for fee-focused visitors.

5. Export HTML from Variant as the design reference → rebuild in Next.js with real interactivity
