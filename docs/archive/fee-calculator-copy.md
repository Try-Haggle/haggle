```
Frequently Asked Questions
```

**Q: How does Haggle charge only 1.5%?**
```
Haggle is protocol infrastructure, not a marketplace. We don't maintain storefronts,
run ads for listings, or employ customer service teams for individual disputes.
AI handles negotiation, smart contracts handle escrow, and stablecoin rails
eliminate payment processor middlemen.
```

**Q: Is the 1.5% fee the only cost?**
```
Yes. 1.5% of the agreed price covers the full Haggle protocol fee.
No listing fees, no payment processing surcharge, no hidden charges.
Shipping costs are separate and transparent — and even the shipping
cost split is negotiable between buyer and seller.
```

**Q: What about buyer protection?**
```
Funds are held in a smart contract escrow until the buyer confirms receipt.
If there's a dispute, Haggle's 3-tier resolution system handles it:
automated rules first, then community voting, then professional arbitration.
```

**Q: How does Haggle compare to Depop's 0% commission?**
```
Depop charges 0% commission but still takes 3.3% + $0.45 in payment processing
fees per transaction. On a $450 sale, that's $15.30.
Haggle's total fee on the same sale is $6.75 — less than half.
Plus, Haggle includes escrow protection and AI negotiation.
```

**Q: Is Haggle only for expensive items?**

```
No. The 1.5% fee works at any price point. A $50 sale costs $0.75 in total fees.
```
