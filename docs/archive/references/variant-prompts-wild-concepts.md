## CONCEPT 6 — "The Heist"

> You're planning a heist — stealing back the money that platforms took from you. The website is a heist planning board.

### Prompt 6-1: The Planning Board

```
A full-screen "heist planning board" pinned to a dark cork board wall.

Center: a blueprint-style floor plan of a vault labeled "eBay HQ — Fee Vault"
Red string connects photos, documents, and sticky notes pinned around it.

Pinned items:
- A mugshot-style photo of the eBay logo: "TARGET: takes 13.25% of every sale"
- A sticky note: "They took $4.2B in fees last year"
- A newspaper clipping: "Seller reports losing $847 in fees over 6 months"
- A floor plan showing: "Entry → Listing Fee → Commission → Payment Processing → Exit (with 80% of your money gone)"

In the corner, a red "CLASSIFIED" stamp.

At the bottom: a manila folder tab that says "THE ESCAPE PLAN →"
When clicked, slides to the next screen.

Vibe: Ocean's Eleven meets a true crime documentary. Gritty, tactile, slightly humorous. Cork board texture, push pins, red string connecting the conspiracy.
```

### Prompt 6-2: The Escape

```
Second screen of the heist concept. Clean break from the cork board — now a sleek getaway screen.

A car dashboard view at night — speedometer, rearview mirror, city lights streaking.
The rearview mirror shows eBay/Poshmark logos getting smaller (you're escaping).

Dashboard readout (like a heads-up display):
- "Fees evaded: $80.98"
- "Money recovered: $438.26 of $450"
- "Getaway vehicle: Haggle Protocol"
- "Status: CLEAN EXIT ✓"

CTA on the dashboard: "Plan your escape → Join Haggle"

Vibe: the satisfying moment after the heist succeeds. Cool, calm, the hard part is over.
```

## CONCEPT 7 — "The Autopsy"

> A clinical, forensic examination of a dead transaction. We dissect where the money went.

### Prompt 7-1: The Examination Table

```
A medical/forensic themed page. Clinical white-blue lighting.

Center: a "$450 transaction" lying on an examination table — visualized as a dollar bill or money stack under a bright surgical light.

A forensic examiner's clipboard on the side reads:
"CAUSE OF DEATH: Fee hemorrhage"
"TIME OF DEATH: At checkout"

Dotted lines and labels pointing to parts of the money, like an autopsy diagram:
- "Commission extraction site — $59.63 removed"
- "Processing wound — $13.35 drained"
- "Promotional tissue damage — $9.00 lost"
- "Shipping laceration — $8.50 bled out"

Remaining vital signs monitor (like a heart rate monitor):
"Seller's pulse: $359.52 — CRITICAL"

Below: "SECOND OPINION AVAILABLE →"
Leading to the Haggle version where the "patient" is healthy:
"Seller's pulse: $438.26 — STABLE ✓"

Vibe: Grey's Anatomy meets infographic. Unsettling but fascinating. The clinical detachment makes the fee extraction feel violent.
```

## CONCEPT 8 — "The Conversation"

> No visuals at all. Just a text conversation loading message by message. Like reading someone's actual negotiation DMs — but it's an AI doing it perfectly.

### Prompt 8-1: The Chat

```
A full-screen iMessage / WhatsApp style chat interface. Nothing else. No header, no nav, no hero.

Messages load one by one with typing indicators (the "..." bubble), as if happening in real time:

[Seller's Agent]: I have an iPhone 15 Pro, A grade. Asking $950.

[Your Agent]: Interesting. Market data shows similar units closing at $800-850 this week. I'd start at $760.

[Seller's Agent]: That's below my floor. I can come down to $890.

[Your Agent]: I see you've had this listed for 6 days with 3 views. The market is cooling. $800 is fair for both of us.

[Seller's Agent]: ...
[Seller's Agent]: $840, and I'll ship within 2 days.

[Your Agent]: $815, buyer covers shipping, 3-day SLA. That gives you $808 after fees.

[Seller's Agent]: ...
[Seller's Agent]: Deal.

[System message in green]: ✅ Agreement reached: $815
Buyer saves: $135 (14.2%)
Seller receives: $808 (vs $827 on eBay for same price)
Time elapsed: 22 seconds

[System]: This negotiation happened automatically. You were eating lunch.

[CTA as a chat bubble]: "Want your own AI negotiator? → Start here"

Vibe: voyeuristic. Like peeking at someone's private messages. The simplicity is the hook — no design, no graphics, just pure dialogue that demonstrates the product. The typing indicators create suspense.
```

## CONCEPT 9 — "The Parallel Universe"

> Split screen. Two parallel timelines of the same sale happening simultaneously — one on eBay, one on Haggle. Every step diverges more.

### Prompt 9-1: The Split

```
A perfectly split screen — left half and right half, running simultaneously.

Left (gray/red tint): "TIMELINE A: eBay"
Right (dark/green tint): "TIMELINE B: Haggle"

Both start identical: "You have an iPhone 15 Pro to sell. $450."

Then they diverge, step by step (scroll to progress):

STEP 1 — LISTING
Left: "Take 12 photos. Write title with keywords. Set category. Enable promoted listing ($9 extra). Wait."
Right: "Describe your item to the AI. Done."

STEP 2 — THE WAIT
Left: "Day 1... Day 3... Day 6. One lowball offer: $280. You decline."
Right: "3 matching buyers found in 4 seconds. AI begins negotiating."

STEP 3 — THE DEAL
Left: "Day 9. Someone offers $430. You accept because you're tired of waiting."
Right: "Round 3. AI closes at $448. Both parties satisfied."

STEP 4 — PAYMENT
Left: "$430 - $56.98 (eBay) - $12.77 (PayPal) = $360.25. Arrives in 3-5 business days."
Right: "$448 - $6.72 (Haggle) = $441.28. In your wallet in 2 seconds."

STEP 5 — THE RESULT
Left: big red number: "$360.25" with "9 days of your time"
Right: big green number: "$441.28" with "22 seconds"

Difference: "+$81.03 and 8 days, 23 hours, 59 minutes, 38 seconds of your life back."

CTA: "Choose your timeline → Start with Haggle"

Vibe: multiverse movie. The split should feel cinematic — like watching two realities play out. The left side progressively gets more gray/depressing while the right side gets brighter.
```

## CONCEPT 10 — "The Slot Machine"

> You pull a lever and it randomly shows how much different platforms would take from your sale. Haggle's result always wins.

### Prompt 10-1: The Machine

```
A full-screen retro slot machine / gacha machine in the center of the screen.

Three spinning reels:
- Reel 1: Platform logos (eBay, Mercari, Poshmark, StockX, Depop, Haggle)
- Reel 2: Fee percentage (13%, 10%, 20%, 15%, 8%, 1.5%)
- Reel 3: "You receive" amount ($369, $388, $352, $374, $409, $443)

A big lever on the right side. "PULL →"

Each pull randomizes Reels 1+2+3 together (matched sets).
The reels spin slot-machine style and land with a satisfying click.

If it lands on any competitor: the machine flashes red, alarm sound suggestion, "OUCH" text.
If it lands on Haggle: green glow, coins pour out animation, "JACKPOT" text.

Below the machine: a running counter — "Total fees lost to platforms today: $2,847,391" (ticking up in real-time).

After 3 pulls, a message: "Tired of gambling with your margins? → The sure bet is Haggle"

Vibe: Las Vegas meets Silicon Valley critique. Colorful, noisy, fun — but with a sharp point: selling on other platforms is literally gambling with your money. Only one result is consistently good.
```

## CONCEPT 11 — "The Weighing Scale"

> An old-fashioned balance scale. One side has your item, the other has what you actually receive. On competitors it's unbalanced. On Haggle it's nearly even.

### Prompt 11-1: The Scale

```
Center of screen: a beautiful brass balance scale, ornate and vintage.

Left pan: Your item (iPhone, sneakers, watch — rotating showcase)
Right pan: Stack of money representing what you receive

Start state: eBay
- Left: iPhone ($450 tag)
- Right: Small money stack ($369)
- Scale tips heavily to the LEFT (item worth more than you get)
- Fee ghosts float away from the right pan: "$59 eBay", "$13 PayPal"...
- The imbalance is visually uncomfortable

User clicks/scrolls to switch platforms:
- Mercari: Right pan slightly higher, but still unbalanced ($388)
- Poshmark: Even worse ($352), scale tips dramatically
- Depop: Better but still off ($409)

Finally: Haggle
- Right pan rises to nearly match left ($443)
- Scale reaches near-perfect balance
- A gentle golden glow emanates from the balanced state
- Text: "This is what fair trade looks like."

CTA: "Balance your next sale → Sell on Haggle"

Vibe: antique, timeless, elegant. The scale is a universal symbol of fairness — Haggle's core value. No tech jargon, no crypto talk. Just a scale that's finally balanced.
```

## CONCEPT 12 — "The X-Ray"

> The website has an X-ray mode. Hover over any competitor's "sell" button and see the hidden fees inside.

### Prompt 12-1: The X-Ray Scanner

```
A landing page showing 5 familiar marketplace interfaces side by side — eBay, Mercari, Poshmark, Depop, StockX. They look normal at first.

A circular "X-ray scanner" follows your mouse cursor — like a magnifying glass but with a blue/green tint.

When you hover over any marketplace's "Sell" or "List Item" button with the scanner:
- The button becomes transparent
- Inside, you see the hidden fee structure rendered like an X-ray/skeleton:
  - Bones labeled: "13.25% commission", "2.9% processing", "promoted listings"
  - A meter showing total extraction: "~16% of your sale"

When you hover over Haggle's "Sell" button:
- The X-ray shows almost nothing inside — clean, empty
- Just a tiny label: "1.5% total"
- The bone structure is minimal and healthy

Headline: "See What's Really Inside That Sell Button"
Subhead: "Every platform hides fees behind a clean UI. Hover to reveal the truth."

After scanning all platforms, CTA: "The only button with nothing to hide → Sell on Haggle"

Vibe: investigative, revealing, "we're exposing the industry." The X-ray aesthetic (blue tint, skeletal/wireframe view) should feel like you're seeing something you weren't supposed to see.
```
